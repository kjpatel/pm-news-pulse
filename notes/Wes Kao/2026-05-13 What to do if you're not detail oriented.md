# What to do if you're not "detail oriented"

**Source**: [Wes Kao](https://newsletter.weskao.com/p/detail-oriented)
**Author**: Wes Kao | **Date**: May 13, 2026

---

## Summary

If you're not naturally detail-oriented, you can't use that as an excuse to ship sloppy work—instead, you should develop systems and habits to catch errors, since your weaknesses shouldn't become your manager's or customers' burden.

## Key Takeaways

- **Adopt quality checklists and systems** like Wes's approach (Gmail clear formatting button, review processes before sending) to compensate for weaker detail orientation rather than accepting it as fixed.
- **Frame your work as if signing your personal name** to every deliverable, which shifts your mindset from "my manager will catch this" to taking ownership of quality and protecting your reputation.
- **Don't pass your weaknesses downstream** to managers or customers to fix—if you want more responsibility, you must demonstrate you can produce generally error-free work without constant oversight.
- **Improve weaknesses selectively, not universally**—focus on which gaps actually block your advancement in your specific role rather than trying to become perfect in all areas.
