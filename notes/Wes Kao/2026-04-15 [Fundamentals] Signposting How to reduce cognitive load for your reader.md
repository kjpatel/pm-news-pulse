# [Fundamentals] Signposting: How to reduce cognitive load for your reader

**Source**: [Wes Kao](https://newsletter.weskao.com/p/fundamentals-signposting)
**Author**: Wes Kao | **Date**: Apr 15, 2026

---

## Summary

Signposting—using preview phrases to guide readers and listeners through your message—reduces cognitive load and improves comprehension by telling audiences what to focus on before revealing the information itself.

## Key Takeaways

- **Start sentences with signposting phrases** like 'The most important part is...' or 'The surprising thing is...' to preview information before delivering it, making content easier to skim and follow
- **Apply signposting to verbal communication** in meetings by using phrases such as 'First... Second...' or 'The reason this matters is...' to help listeners refocus when attention drifts
- **Go beyond formatting alone**—while headers and visual structure help, the real power of signposting comes from the language and logic of your content itself, not just document layout
- **Audit your recent communications** by reviewing a memo or presentation and identifying where signposting phrases could clarify your message and reduce questions from confused audiences
