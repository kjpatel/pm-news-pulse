# Speak in the affirmative: "Do this" versus "Don't do that"

**Source**: [Wes Kao](https://newsletter.weskao.com/p/speak-in-the-affirmative-do-this)
**Author**: Wes Kao | **Date**: Apr 29, 2026

---

## Summary

Speaking in affirmative language ("do this") rather than negative language ("don't do that") reduces cognitive load for your audience and improves both clarity and emotional tone in communication. This simple shift makes your feedback more constructive, your requests clearer, and your positioning more confident.

## Key Takeaways

- **Replace negatives with affirmatives** when giving feedback or making requests—say "Point your toes" instead of "Don't flex your feet" to reduce the mental step required for understanding and to avoid sounding reprimanding.
- **Fill the void when you must use negatives** by pairing what you don't want with what you do want—e.g., "Don't disappear for three weeks; share progress in a few days" prevents your recipient from filling the gap with an interpretation you disagree with.
- **Own your constraints confidently** by stating what you can do rather than what you can't—say "I can meet between 11-3pm" instead of "I can only meet between 11-3pm" to avoid sounding insecure or apologetic.
- **Use visualization to shape behavior** by giving airtime to the positive outcome you want—what you emphasize expands in your recipient's mind, so focus on the direction you want them to go rather than what to avoid.
