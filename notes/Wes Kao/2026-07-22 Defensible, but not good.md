# Defensible, but not good

**Source**: [Wes Kao](https://newsletter.weskao.com/p/defensible-but-not-good)
**Author**: Wes Kao | **Date**: Jul 22, 2026

---

## Summary

The article argues that work can meet all requirements and appear professional while still missing the mark on actual value, and emphasizes the importance of using judgment and maintaining intellectual honesty rather than hiding behind defensible but mediocre outcomes.

## Key Takeaways

- **Distinguish between defensible and good** - Stop assuming metrics staying flat means your work is acceptable; use your judgment and eyes to identify poor quality even when data doesn't show dramatic drops.
- **Don't wait for crisis signals** - Rather than waiting for abandonment rates to spike or customers to complain, proactively improve products and processes using creativity and taste, especially when you have limited resources for A/B testing.
- **Apply intellectual honesty to decision-making** - For each initiative competing for finite budget and headcount, have conviction that it's the right bet rather than settling for 'decent' ideas that don't move the needle.
- **Use judgment over process-hiding** - Avoid using the excuse of needing more data or following processes to justify inaction; make decisions based on competent analysis and your perspective, even with incomplete information.
