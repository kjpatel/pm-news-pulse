# Your product has a new user. It’s not human.

**Source**: [Elena's Growth Scoop](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/your-product-has-a-new-user-its-not)
**Author**: Elena Verna | **Date**: Apr 22, 2026

---

## Summary

As AI agents increasingly interact with products on behalf of humans through protocols like MCP, companies must fundamentally rethink their ICP from 'human users' to 'AI agents,' which requires opening up product interfaces, reconsidering what actually defensible, and redesigning growth strategies around agent-friendly documentation and reliability rather than human UX.

## Key Takeaways

- **Expose your product through MCP or equivalent:** If agents can't access your functionality through structured, machine-consumable protocols, you'll be excluded from workflows entirely. Prioritize clean, well-documented APIs and endpoints that agents can discover and integrate with.
- **Evaluate your actual moat:** Much of what companies thought was defensibility (UI complexity, training requirements, friction) was just stickiness through pain. Ask yourself: if an agent can replicate your core functionality without touching your product, what genuinely cannot be easily replicated? (data, network effects, deep integrations, etc.)
- **Flip your growth strategy from distribution to integration:** Stop optimizing for 'getting users into your product' and start optimizing for 'how does our product fit into their workflow?' Invest in documentation, predictable outputs, and reliability over ads, funnels, and onboarding completion rates.
- **Accept the market will split into two categories:** Some products will optimize for agent invisibility (infrastructure/API-first), while others will double down on human enjoyment and brand affinity (minimum lovable products). Decide which strategy matches your competitive advantages.
- **Prepare for frictionless evaluation and replacement:** Agents don't care about switching costs or brand loyalty—they evaluate purely on output quality and speed. Your customer retention will depend entirely on consistently outperforming alternatives, not habit or complexity.
