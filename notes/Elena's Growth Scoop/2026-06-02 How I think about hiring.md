# How I think about hiring

**Source**: [Elena's Growth Scoop](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/hiring-lovable-cowboys-farmers-old-guard-new-guard)
**Author**: Elena Verna | **Date**: Jun 02, 2026

---

## Summary

Building high-velocity teams requires intentionally hiring a balanced mix of 'cowboys' (innovators) and 'farmers' (operators), paired with both experienced industry veterans and AI-native newcomers, along with deliberate cultural practices that enable these diverse groups to collaborate effectively.

## Key Takeaways

- **Create a hiring matrix** that explicitly balances cowboys vs. farmers and old guard vs. new guard rather than defaulting to hiring only operators; ensure every team has representation across all quadrants to maintain healthy tension between innovation and scalability.
- **Use trial work and contract-to-hire arrangements** for all levels (not just entry-level) to evaluate cultural fit and actual working style over 2-3 days, since traditional interviews can't reveal whether someone is truly a cowboy, farmer, or which guard they belong to.
- **Build a flat hierarchy with minimal titles** to reveal who genuinely needs structure versus who is motivated by ego, enabling cross-generational and cross-type collaboration by removing status-based barriers to working together.
- **Hire for humility as a core trait** because the success of diverse team composition depends on mutual appreciation and respect across different working styles, not just having the right mix of personalities.
- **Pair old cowboys with young farmers as high-impact IC roles** to create positions that benefit from both AI literacy and business experience, maximizing impact when someone bridges innovation with organizational freedom and scalability.
