# Your AI strategy has a trust problem, not a tooling problem

**Source**: [Elena's Growth Scoop](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/your-ai-strategy-has-a-trust-problem)
**Author**: Elena Verna | **Date**: Jun 05, 2026

---

## Summary

Organizations are hindering AI innovation not through lack of tools, but through organizational structures built on distrust that prevent employees from having the autonomy and access needed to move quickly. The solution requires fundamentally restructuring how companies distribute information and decision-making authority rather than simply implementing new AI tools.

## Key Takeaways

- **Unlock employee agency** - Grant high-performing individuals decision-making authority and unrestricted access to information regardless of title, enabling them to move at AI-speed without waiting for approval chains.
- **Flatten information hierarchies** - Stop gating critical context behind manager titles and status theater; employees need the same information quality as AI systems to make sound decisions and iterate rapidly.
- **Prototype organizational changes** - Test flatter structures with R&D or innovation teams first rather than attempting org-wide transformation; establish aggressive targets and measure what becomes possible with genuine autonomy.
- **Reduce middle management friction** - Recognize that traditional middle managers primarily route information up/down chains; eliminate this bottleneck by enabling direct information flow and cross-functional coordination among individual contributors.
- **Prepare employees for responsibility** - Acknowledge that many workers trained in command-and-control environments may need support and possibly replacement; help people understand that autonomy comes with real accountability for outcomes.
