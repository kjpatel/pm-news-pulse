# The Minimill of AI

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/using-local-ai-to-work-faster/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Jun 05, 2026

---

## Summary

Local AI models on devices like laptops can handle routine tasks efficiently while routing complex work to the cloud, creating a distributed 'minimill' architecture that mirrors Nucor's disruptive steel mill model and could fundamentally reshape enterprise AI spending.

## Key Takeaways

- **Implement hybrid task routing** - Use local models to classify and handle straightforward tasks (scheduling, email triage, CRM updates) while reserving cloud compute for genuinely complex work, improving throughput by 25% and reducing queue time by 94%.
- **Adopt edge-first infrastructure** - Deploy distilled AI models on employee devices (laptops, phones) to handle the easy 80% of work locally, paying cloud rates only for the hard 20%, dramatically reducing hyperscaler costs.
- **Plan for distributed minimill proliferation** - Prepare your organization for tens of millions of edge devices running their own task routing agents in the coming years, similar to how Nucor's minimills displaced integrated steel giants.
