# The $112 Billion Quarter

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/2026-04-29-the-112-billion-quarter-hyperscalers-bet-the-farm-on-ai/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Apr 29, 2026

---

## Summary

Google Cloud's 63% YoY growth significantly outpaces AWS (28%) and Azure (40%) in Q1 2026, driven by enterprise AI adoption and vertical integration of models and TPUs, while all three hyperscalers collectively invested $112 billion in quarterly infrastructure spending.

## Key Takeaways

- **Prioritize vertical integration**: Google's ownership of Gemini and TPUs without licensing fees creates a structural profitability advantage—companies should evaluate building versus buying critical infrastructure layers.
- **Capitalize on AI-driven capacity constraints**: Google's compute constraint resulted in a $460B backlog; businesses can exploit demand surges by rapidly scaling infrastructure to meet explosive AI workload growth.
- **Monitor token consumption acceleration**: Enterprise customers are outpacing initial AI budget commitments by 45%—plan for exponential usage growth once AI models deploy to production rather than linear projections.
- **Leverage debt strategically for infrastructure races**: Google and Amazon are raising record debt ($32B and $54B respectively) to fund capex buildouts, signaling that financing infrastructure gaps through capital markets is becoming standard competitive practice.
- **Secure long-term AI capacity commitments**: The emergence of $100M-$1B+ cloud deals with multi-year fulfillment windows means enterprises and vendors must lock in pricing and capacity agreements now before 2027 constraints intensify.
