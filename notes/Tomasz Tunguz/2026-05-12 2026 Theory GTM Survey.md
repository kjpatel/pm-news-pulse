## Summary

Tomasz Tunguz is launching the 2026 Annual Theory Go-to-Market Survey to understand how startups have evolved their GTM strategies over the past several years, with a focus on five testable hypotheses about AI's impact on sales teams, buyer behavior, and efficiency gains.

## Key Takeaways

- **Test augmented rep effectiveness** - Design experiments comparing augmented human-AI teams versus autonomous AI systems versus unaugmented humans to validate which approach delivers superior conversion rates and productivity.
- **Measure AI's impact on performance disparity** - Track whether top-quartile GTM teams investing in AI are widening efficiency and conversion gaps against bottom-quartile teams, and quantify the actual performance spread.
- **Prioritize buyer-side AI dynamics** - Shift focus from seller-side AI tools to understanding how buyer adoption of AI (automated RFPs, AI-assisted evaluations) lengthens sales cycles and creates new objections.
- **Track headcount impact over cost savings** - Monitor whether AI productivity gains are being captured as flatter SDR hiring rather than revenue growth, making headcount trends the true metric of AI ROI.
- **Recalibrate AI expectations downward** - Reset internal assumptions about AI efficiency gains from the overly optimistic 500% improvements projected in 2024 to realistic, measurable targets based on actual 2026 data.
