# Three Years In

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/three-years-in/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Jul 10, 2026

---

## Summary

Theory Ventures reflects on three years of backing AI-native companies, showing how AI has compressed software development timelines, fragmented infrastructure markets (especially inference), and enabled smaller teams to operate with unprecedented leverage. The firm argues that venture stage definitions are obsolete, open-source models now compete with frontier models, and the next wave of opportunity lies in operationalizing intelligence through specialized infrastructure and agentic systems.

## Key Takeaways

- **Redefine venture stages by maturity, not funding size** — Seed rounds now range from $1M to $500M, making traditional category labels meaningless; instead, classify companies by their actual product-market maturity and capability to compound.
- **Focus on inference infrastructure specialization** — Like databases fragmented into OLTP, OLAP, and vector systems, inference is now segmenting by workload (video, batch, local, agentic, real-time); build or invest in operators that serve constrained use cases efficiently.
- **Deploy AI security defenses at the pace of threats** — MCP servers, plugins, and agents create exponential attack surfaces; invest in AI-native security (alert triage, vulnerability assessment, agent-level hardening) that matches attacker speed measured in minutes, not months.
- **Operationalize AI inside legacy back-office systems** — ERP and operations have resisted change for decades due to messy data and switching costs; agents enable internal transformation without rip-and-replace, creating opportunities to modernize unglamorous but valuable work.
- **Build technical venture firms with AI leverage** — Smaller teams (3 investors + 9 intelligence staff at Theory) can analyze 2x deal flow through AI agents that map markets and source companies early; ensure every team member uses the technology they invest in for shared fluency.
