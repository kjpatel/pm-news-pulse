# The AI Skepticism Map

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/ai-shorts/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz (featuring Michael Burry and Leopold Aschenbrenner) | **Date**: Jun 01, 2026

---

## Summary

The financial markets show nuanced skepticism about AI, with short-selling concentrated in smaller software and developer tools companies rather than hyperscalers or semiconductor makers, reflecting investor doubts about whether these businesses can prove their worth in the AI era.

## Key Takeaways

- **Monitor short interest trends by segment**: GPU data center businesses have seen 60% growth in short shares year-over-year, while hyperscalers remain lightly shorted at 1.1%, signaling differentiated market confidence across AI infrastructure tiers.
- **Watch for capital and demand risks**: The highest short interest concentrates in mid-cap companies dependent on future capital access or demand validation, not in proven infrastructure plays like NVIDIA (1.2% short) or memory makers like Micron.
- **Recognize the memory inflection**: Semiconductor short-selling decreased as memory and storage emerged as bottleneck resources, with memory makers becoming trillion-dollar companies—a critical shift in AI infrastructure priorities.
- **Evaluate software's AI transition risk**: Developer tools and enterprise SaaS face rising short interest (9.5% and 8.9% respectively) as markets question whether these businesses can prove operational value in an AI-native world.
