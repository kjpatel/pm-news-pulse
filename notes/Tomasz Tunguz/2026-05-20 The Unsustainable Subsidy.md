# The Unsustainable Subsidy

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/ai-model-inflation/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: May 20, 2026

---

## Summary

AI vendors are shifting pricing strategies based on financial pressures, with Google maintaining the lowest costs while Anthropic and OpenAI have increased prices as capital expenditures reach record levels.

## Key Takeaways

- **Monitor vendor pricing patterns** - Track AI model pricing quarterly to identify when vendors shift from market-share focus to margin optimization, signaling potential margin pressures in the industry.
- **Evaluate total cost of ownership** - Compare input and output token costs across vendors (Google at $2-$12/1M, Anthropic at $5-$25/1M, OpenAI at $5-$30/1M) to optimize your AI infrastructure spend across use cases.
- **Anticipate pricing increases** - As capex spending continues to rise across all three major vendors, prepare for potential price increases by locking in current rates or diversifying across multiple model providers.
- **Understand strategic intent behind pricing** - Recognize that pricing cuts signal vendor focus on market share during cash-rich periods, while increases indicate margin recovery during high capex cycles.
