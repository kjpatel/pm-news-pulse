# AI Engineering Productivity is Anything But Normal

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/ai-engineering-productivity-anything-but-normal/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Jul 21, 2026

---

## Summary

AI engineering productivity gains vary dramatically across three tiers: modest 20-46% improvements from basic IDE distribution, 2.5-3x gains from companies building agent operating layers, and 8x+ improvements from those treating agents as first-class organizational units. The gap isn't about the AI model itself but the operational discipline built around it.

## Key Takeaways

- **Audit your current tier**: Most teams experience only 20-30% productivity gains from distributing AI tools unchanged. Measure your baseline with the same rigor as Google's RCT (~100 engineers) or Faros's approach (tracking epic completion, bug rates, and review velocity) to identify where you actually stand.
- **Build harnesses around agents**: The 2.5-3x frontier tier requires orchestrating AI agents across your existing tools (GitHub, Linear, Slack) with escalation workflows. This isn't about replacing engineers but structuring how agents share context and judgment—following Replit's manager-agent model that drops PR review time 30% and support handling 60%.
- **Treat agents as organizational units**: To reach 8x efficiency (factory tier), deploy agents like Devin for large-scale refactoring as first-class team members with measurable outputs. Nubank's 8x gain and 20x cost reduction show this isn't theoretical—start with your monolithic codebases or high-touch support work.
- **Measure quality alongside velocity**: Don't replicate the default tier's trap (66% faster epics but 54% more bugs). Track bugs per developer, reversion rates, and incident counts as you scale—Replit and Amplitude kept these flat while tripling output.
- **Expect 3x as the new baseline**: The frontier is no longer exceptional. Set engineering leadership expectations at 3x productivity, not 20-30%, and build the operating discipline (harnesses, agent loops, context sharing) to deliver it within 6 months.
