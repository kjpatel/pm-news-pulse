# What If There Is No Moat Yet?

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/what-if-there-is-no-moat/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Jun 26, 2026

---

## Summary

Application-layer companies don't need leading moats at founding—they earn moats through execution, scale, and brand over time, whereas infrastructure companies require defensible technical differentiation from the start to justify their capital intensity.

## Key Takeaways

- **Distinguish between leading and lagging moats**: Leading moats (technical differentiation, proprietary datasets) exist at founding and are essential for infrastructure companies, while lagging moats (scale economies, brand, switching costs) are earned through years of execution and are sufficient for application companies.
- **Focus on execution over engineering at the app layer**: Application founders should prioritize market focus, rapid execution, and moving faster than incumbents rather than seeking immediate technical defensibility, as real moats will emerge from volume and embedded customers.
- **Be honest about moat development timelines**: The realistic answer for application startups is "we are building one"—moats like Salesforce's sales muscle and brand dominance or Slack's network effects take years to develop and shouldn't be claimed prematurely in pitch decks.
- **Match infrastructure strategy to capital requirements**: Infrastructure companies like Snowflake must have leading moats (e.g., separated storage/compute architecture) upfront because they require substantial research and capital—application companies can rely on execution and market dynamics instead.
