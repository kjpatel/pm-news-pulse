# Competitive Strategy in the Age of AI

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/competitive-strategy-in-ai/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Apr 24, 2026

---

## Summary

Anthropic is executing Google's proven competitive strategy of commoditizing complementary products to drive core product adoption and data accumulation, launching free tools like MCP, Claude Code, Cowork, and Design to entrench Claude as the central AI platform.

## Key Takeaways

- **Identify your complement**: Map out which adjacent products (data integration, security, design, file management) are tollbooths preventing users from fully leveraging your core product, then build free versions to remove friction.
- **Build the flywheel**: Each free complement drives more usage of your core product, which generates more data to improve it, creating a self-reinforcing competitive moat that's difficult for startups to challenge.
- **Expect category extinction**: Recognize that commoditizing complements kills entire market categories that were previously viable; focus your startup on specialization or direct competition rather than competing in newly commoditized spaces.
- **Outfocus the giant**: As a startup, your advantage isn't speed or resources but the ability to maintain laser focus on one problem where you can build something demonstrably better than a free, good-enough alternative from a larger player.
