# The Beginning of Scarcity in AI

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/ai-compute-crisis-2026/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Apr 13, 2026

---

## Summary

AI infrastructure has shifted from abundance to scarcity, with GPU prices surging 48% in two months and major AI companies limiting access to cutting-edge models, fundamentally reshaping competitive dynamics for startups and enterprises alike.

## Key Takeaways

- **Monitor GPU pricing trends** - Track CoreWeave and Nvidia Blackwell pricing weekly, as rental costs and contract minimums are rising rapidly (up 48% in two months), directly impacting your AI infrastructure budget and ROI calculations.
- **Plan for model access limitations** - Assume state-of-the-art models will be gated behind relationship-based selling; build contingency strategies using smaller models, open-source alternatives, or on-premise deployments now before scarcity deepens.
- **Invest in margin management discipline** - Procurement and cost optimization will become core competitive advantages; audit your compute spending, negotiate long-term contracts before prices rise further, and model scenarios for 3-year contract commitments.
- **Diversify your model strategy** - Don't rely solely on frontier models; evaluate smaller models, alternative providers (Claude, Gemini, open-source), and hybrid approaches to reduce dependency on scarce SOTA capacity and mitigate pricing risk.
- **Lock in capacity early** - If you depend on GPU infrastructure, secure multi-year contracts and relationships with providers now; waiting will mean higher prices and less favorable terms as demand compounds against fixed supply.
