# Observations on Writing with AI

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/observations-on-writing-with-ai/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: May 18, 2026

---

## Summary

AI struggles with writing because each model has its own distinct voice, and using multiple AI systems for editing creates conflicting visions rather than coherent improvement. Authentic writing is defined by intentional imperfections that AI cannot replicate.

## Key Takeaways

- **Recognize AI's voice limitations**: Each AI model (Gemini, Claude, OpenAI Codex) has a distinct voice and personality—understand these differences before using them for editorial feedback.
- **Avoid the AI editorial council trap**: Multiple AI systems reviewing the same work create conflicting tones and styles rather than polished output; single-model iteration works better than multi-model consensus.
- **Embrace imperfection as authenticity**: Intentional imperfections—vinyl pops, punctuation quirks, imperfect analogies—are what distinguish human writing; focus on preserving these rather than over-polishing to AI standards.
- **Use AI for specific tasks, not voice**: Deploy AI for documentation, code review logic, and structural feedback, but rely on human judgment for maintaining authentic voice and style across longer pieces.
