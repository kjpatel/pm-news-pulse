# The Three Questions in AI Sales

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/your-margin-is-my-software-opportunity/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Apr 28, 2026

---

## Summary

AI sales strategy has fundamentally shifted from asking about software budgets to asking about the labor-to-software ratio companies want to achieve in three years, transforming the conversation into strategic planning about AI-driven labor compression. This reframe positions AI not as a software expense but as a tool that can fundamentally restructure departmental economics.

## Key Takeaways

- **Ask the third question**: Move beyond "What's your software budget?" to "What do you want your labor-to-software ratio to be in three years?" to shift from transactional to strategic conversations.
- **Target high-ratio departments**: Prioritize sales efforts toward departments with the highest current labor-to-software ratios (Sales at 10:1, Engineering at 25:1) where AI compression creates the largest opportunities.
- **Use task coverage data**: Reference task coverage percentages from the Anthropic Economic Index (e.g., customer service at 70%, computer/math at 36%) to quantify compression potential and justify larger labor budget reallocation.
- **Execute two-step expansion**: Land initial deals by justifying ROI against existing software spend, then expand by positioning AI as a way to capture labor cost savings and shift budget ratios downward.
- **Reframe the ceiling**: Help buyers understand that software budget isn't the ceiling for AI spend—it's the floor, since the real value comes from labor cost reduction, not software replacement.
