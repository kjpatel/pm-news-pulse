# Google's Cloud Revenue Converges to NVIDIA's Growth Rate

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/google-cloud-hyperscaler-to-hardware/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Jul 22, 2026

---

## Summary

Google Cloud's 82% YoY revenue growth now converges with NVIDIA's Data Center growth rate, driven by massive demand for AI compute rental, while the company simultaneously begins selling TPU hardware and achieves record 35.6% operating margins.

## Key Takeaways

- **Monitor the margin convergence**: Google Cloud's operating margin expanded 14.9 points YoY to 35.6%, nearly closing the gap with AWS's 37.7% margin—track whether Google can achieve cost leadership in cloud infrastructure as AI workloads scale.
- **Track the $514B backlog conversion**: With over half of Google Cloud's record $514B backlog converting to revenue within 24 months, use this visibility to forecast sustained 80%+ growth rates and capital allocation priorities across hyperscalers.
- **Prepare for the $1T+ capex cycle**: Alphabet, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, and Oracle are collectively spending well beyond $1T on AI infrastructure in 2027—evaluate how this capital intensity affects unit economics and competitive positioning for AI infrastructure vendors.
- **Distinguish rental vs. hardware revenue models**: Google's TPU system sales contributed minimally to Q2 growth (bulk lands in 2027), while NVIDIA's chip sales dominate—understand how vertical integration strategies differ between rental-focused (Google) and component-focused (NVIDIA) players.
