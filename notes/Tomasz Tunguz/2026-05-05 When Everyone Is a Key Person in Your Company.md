# When Everyone Is a Key Person in Your Company

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/labor-ai-spend-ratio-eng-team/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: May 05, 2026

## Summary

As startups adopt AI agents to reduce headcount, they face a critical tradeoff between efficiency and resiliency: highly AI-dependent teams concentrate institutional knowledge in few people, creating single points of failure similar to running a factory at 100% utilization.

## Key Takeaways

- **Avoid the 90/10 trap**: Running a team with 90% AI and 10% labor creates dangerous knowledge concentration. Most startups should maintain higher human redundancy until AI systems mature.
- **Apply manufacturing wisdom**: Just as factories run optimally at 70-90% utilization, engineering teams need organizational slack. That slack prevents cascading failures when key people leave.
- **Reassess your AI/labor ratio**: Before shifting to agent-heavy teams, calculate the real cost of losing institutional knowledge (prompt engineering, agent validation, debugging) versus the throughput gains.
