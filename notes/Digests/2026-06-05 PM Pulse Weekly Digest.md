## This Week

**Trust structures and org design matter more than AI tooling; the real competitive edge is in how you distribute decision-making and build for edge-first architectures.**

This week reveals a critical inversion: AI capability is now table-stakes, but organizational structure determines execution speed. Three themes converge: first, trust and autonomy constraints outweigh tool limitations—companies fail on AI not from missing Claude or GPT, but from hoarding information and decision authority. Second, efficiency metrics are displacing raw performance; 'intelligence per dollar' and edge-first models (local + cloud hybrid) will reshape how enterprises spend. Third, the PM role itself is shifting from spec-first planning to artifact-first validation as agents ship prototypes faster than orgs can align. For scaling product orgs, this means your hiring, trust model, and discovery process are now your competitive moat—not your stack.

- Org structure is your AI constraint, not tooling — fix trust and autonomy before adding more platforms
- Edge + cloud hybrid architectures are emerging as the default enterprise AI model — efficiency per dollar beats pure performance
- PMs must shift from spec-first discovery to artifact-first validation — code prototypes now validate faster than wireframes or documents
- Hiring now requires intentional mixing of cowboys, farmers, AI natives, and veterans — culture practices become as critical as skill sets

Elena Verna argues that most organizations fail at AI not due to tool limitations, but because distrust and centralized decision-making prevent employees from acting autonomously. The fix isn't better AI tools—it's restructuring information access and decision rights to enable speed. **Why it matters**: Directly addresses the org design blocker preventing AI adoption; essential for VP planning team structure and decision-making authority

- **Flatten information hierarchies** - Stop gating critical context behind manager titles and status theater; employees need the same information quality as AI systems to make sound decisions and iterate rapidly.
- **Prototype organizational changes** - Test flatter structures with R&D or innovation teams first rather than attempting org-wide transformation; establish aggressive targets and measure what becomes possible with genuine autonomy.
- **Reduce middle management friction** - Recognize that traditional middle managers primarily route information up/down chains; eliminate this bottleneck by enabling direct information flow and cross-functional coordination among individual contributors.
- **Prepare employees for responsibility** - Acknowledge that many workers trained in command-and-control environments may need support and possibly replacement; help people understand that autonomy comes with real accountability for outcomes.

- **Hire for humility as a core trait** because the success of diverse team composition depends on mutual appreciation and respect across different working styles, not just having the right mix of personalities.
- **Pair old cowboys with young farmers as high-impact IC roles** to create positions that benefit from both AI literacy and business experience, maximizing impact when someone bridges innovation with organizational freedom and scalability.
