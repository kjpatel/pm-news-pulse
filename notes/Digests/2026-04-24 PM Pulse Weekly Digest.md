## This Week

**PMs who don't learn to build will be displaced by those who do—and your product strategy must account for AI agents as first-class users, not afterthoughts.**

This week crystallizes a fundamental PM reinvention underway. Three distinct shifts converge: (1) AI is elevating builder PMs who write code and design workflows over traditional roadmap managers; (2) products must architect for agentic interaction, not just human UX, forcing rethinking of defensibility and growth; (3) enterprise infrastructure—not consumer polish—is becoming the winning playbook. The tension is acute: shipping fast with incomplete products (Anthropic), doubling velocity with AI-paired teams (Intercom), and redesigning ICPs around agents (MCP protocols) all require different org structures and skill sets. For Series C leaders, the implication is stark: either your team evolves rapidly into builders and agentic-first thinkers, or you risk commoditization by teams that do.

- Builder PMs are becoming the competitive moat—coding, automation, and multi-hour autonomous task orchestration are table stakes, not nice-to-haves.
- Agentic products demand new design principles—ICP shifts from human users to AI agents, forcing product teams to rethink defensibility, documentation, and PLG mechanics.
- Enterprise infrastructure beats consumer experience—Anthropic's multi-LLM support, Google Cloud's agent platforms, and semantic BI signal that moats are in integration layers and reliability, not UI.
- Velocity scales with org structure, not just AI—Intercom's 2x gains required telemetry, custom skills with guardrails, and cross-functional shipping (designers and PMs coding), not just throwing Claude at engineers.
