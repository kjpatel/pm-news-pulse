## This Week

**AI is reshaping what product strategy means: as building gets cheaper, taste, positioning, and organizational design become your competitive moat.**

This week crystallizes a fundamental shift in product leadership. AI commoditizes feature velocity, forcing strategy to move upstream—toward positioning clarity, discovery rigor, and organizational design. Four threads dominate: (1) taste and judgment now matter more than specs, as PMs evaluate working prototypes at speed rather than plan in advance; (2) positioning and discovery are breaking down under old assumptions, requiring disciplined frameworks to rebuild; (3) AI-native org structures are delivering 30x productivity gains, creating urgency around team composition; (4) infrastructure constraints will reshape pricing and margins through 2028. For VPs scaling at Series C, this means your competitive advantage shifts from execution speed to strategic clarity, discovery discipline, and organizational leverage.

- Taste at speed replaces lengthy specs — PMs now judge working prototypes in real-time, fundamentally changing how product teams operate and coordinate.
- Discovery and positioning are broken — most companies lack outcome-based goals and bypass validation, creating an opening for disciplined frameworks to become differentiators.
- Org structure is a product lever — AI-native teams achieve 30x productivity per engineer, forcing hard choices about headcount, communication overhead, and decision-making patterns.
- Infrastructure scarcity is real through 2028 — GPU, power, and data center constraints will drive pricing discipline and workload optimization, not commoditization.

### 1. [There's a New PM Skill. It's Called Taste at Speed](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/taste-at-speed)
*Product Growth* — Aakash Gupta — Mar 13, 2026  `#AI Strategy`  `#Product Growth`

As AI commoditizes software development, PMs must shift from lengthy specifications to real-time evaluation of working prototypes, killing inferior versions and shipping the best. This 'taste at speed' becomes the defining PM skill, fundamentally changing how product teams coordinate, prioritize, and operate. For Series C VPs, this reframes what product leadership means: less planning rigor, more judgment velocity.

**Why it matters**: Core shift in how PMs operate: taste and judgment on working software now matter more than pre-build planning—essential for scaling product orgs in AI era

- **Shift from PRDs to prototypes**: Move specifications from the planning phase to after prototype evaluation. Test working software in hours instead of weeks, then spec the survivor rather than specifying before building.
- **Build judgment through volume**: Evaluate 15 prototypes weekly instead of one spec monthly to compound your taste gap over 6 months. Speed creates a flywheel where experience gap becomes taste gap becomes career gap.
- **Embrace the 80% kill rate**: Use AI tools to generate divergent options (5+ parallel prototypes simultaneously), then ruthlessly eliminate weak ideas. Speed without filtering judgment just builds wrong things faster.
- **Adopt cyclical team workflows**: Replace linear Idea→PRD→Design→Build→Ship with cyclical Idea→5 prototypes→Evaluate→Kill 4→Spec→Ship. Organize teams around parallel exploration rather than sequential approval.
- **Democratize building across roles**: Enable PMs, designers, and non-technical staff to generate prototypes using Claude Code and similar tools. This shifts the bottleneck from 'can we build it' to 'should we ship it.'
