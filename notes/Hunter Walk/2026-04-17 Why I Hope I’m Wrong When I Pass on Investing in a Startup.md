# Why I Hope I’m Wrong When I Pass on Investing in a Startup

**Source**: [Hunter Walk](https://hunterwalk.com/2026/04/17/why-i-hope-im-wrong-when-i-pass-on-investing-in-a-startup/)
**Author**: Hunter Walk | **Date**: Apr 17, 2026

---

## Summary

A VC investor argues that when passing on investment opportunities, the healthier mindset is hoping you're wrong and genuinely rooting for the founder's success anyway, rather than hoping your rejection was the right call.

## Key Takeaways

- **Reframe rejection decisions**: When passing on founders, consciously shift your mindset to hope they prove you wrong and build something meaningful, rather than validating your decision to decline.
- **Separate investment capacity from genuine support**: Recognize that most founders excite you personally even if you can't fund them—maintain authentic enthusiasm for their success regardless of financial participation.
- **Assess investor sincerity**: As a founder, pay attention to whether investors' offers to help post-rejection feel genuine or performative by observing their follow-up engagement and actual support.
