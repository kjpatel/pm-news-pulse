# At AI Dinners, You Can Leave Before Dessert. And Other Rules for IRL IS BACK Networking

**Author**: Hunter Walk | **Date**: Apr 18, 2026

## Summary

Hunter Walk provides practical guidelines for hosting effective in-person networking dinners in the AI community, addressing common mistakes first-time event planners make and offering specific recommendations for timing, structure, and attendee experience.

## Key Takeaways

- **Structure your timing** - Allocate 30 minutes for drinks/mingling, complete dinner in 90 minutes on weeknights, and allow people to leave after entrees without social awkwardness.
- **Enable organic conversation** - Keep individual tables to six people maximum, use guided conversation prompts, and avoid forced 'report outs' that turn dinners into seminar-style events.
- **Facilitate post-event connection** - Send attendee lists with contact info after the dinner to help guests follow up, but make sponsor pitches opt-in rather than pushing contacts on attendees.
- **Reduce friction with logistics** - Use name tags or table cards, send prompt schedules with clear timing upfront, and allow flexible seating changes after entrees to accommodate no-shows and early departures.
- **Keep sponsors brief and respectful** - Limit sponsor pitches to 30-60 seconds maximum and avoid aggressive post-dinner sales outreach that commodifies attendee participation.
