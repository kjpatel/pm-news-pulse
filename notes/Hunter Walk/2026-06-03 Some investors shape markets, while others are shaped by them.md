# Some investors shape markets, while others are shaped by them

**Source**: [Hunter Walk](https://hunterwalk.com/2026/06/03/some-investors-shape-markets-while-others-are-shaped-by-them/)
**Author**: Hunter Walk | **Date**: Jun 03, 2026

---

## Summary

VCs should define their strategy based on first principles rather than simply reacting to market pressures and raising larger funds. True competitive advantage comes from identifying your unique playbook and executing it excellently, not from chasing capital size.

## Key Takeaways

- **Start with first principles**: Ask yourself why your firm exists and what playbook you're uniquely suited to execute, rather than defaulting to raising bigger funds because competitors are doing so.
- **Distinguish strategy from reason for existing**: Fund size is a tactical tool to execute your strategy, not the foundational reason your firm should exist—conflating these leads to mediocrity.
- **Audit your resources strategically**: Evaluate what resources you already have, what you need to acquire, and what you need to shed to execute your chosen strategy with excellence.
- **Resist commoditization pressure**: The fact that most VCs are reacting identically to market changes indicates capital is becoming commoditized—differentiation requires independent thinking and conviction.
