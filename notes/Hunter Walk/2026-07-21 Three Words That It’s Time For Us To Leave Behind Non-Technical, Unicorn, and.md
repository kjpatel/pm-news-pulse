# Three Words That It’s Time For Us To Leave Behind: Non-Technical, Unicorn, and Oversubscribed.

**Source**: [Hunter Walk](https://hunterwalk.com/2026/07/21/three-words-that-its-time-for-us-to-leave-behind-non-technical-unicorn-and-oversubscribed/)
**Author**: Hunter Walk | **Date**: Jul 21, 2026

---

## Summary

Three outdated terms—'non-technical,' 'unicorn,' and 'oversubscribed'—no longer serve meaningful purposes in tech and should be abandoned. AI has eliminated the validity of non-technical roles, unicorn valuations have become misleading metrics for success, and oversubscribed is manufactured jargon that adds no real value.

## Key Takeaways

- **Reject the non-technical label** – AI tools and no-code platforms mean everyone in your organization should develop technical literacy; being non-technical is now a choice, not a role requirement.
- **Stop obsessing over valuation milestones** – Focus your strategy and messaging on product quality, business model, and company performance rather than chasing arbitrary $1B+ valuation benchmarks that incentivize neither founders nor media.
- **Eliminate 'oversubscribed' from your vocabulary** – The term is meaningless jargon that obscures rather than clarifies funding round reality; use concrete metrics instead.
