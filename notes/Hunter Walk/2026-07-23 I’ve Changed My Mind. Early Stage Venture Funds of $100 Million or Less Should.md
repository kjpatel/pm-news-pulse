# I’ve Changed My Mind. Early Stage Venture Funds of $100 Million or Less Should Hold Almost No Reserves for Follow-On.

**Source**: [Hunter Walk](https://hunterwalk.com/2026/07/23/ive-changed-my-mind-early-stage-venture-funds-of-100-million-or-less-should-hold-almost-no-reserves-for-follow-on/)
**Author**: Hunter Walk | **Date**: Jul 23, 2026

---

## Summary

Hunter Walk argues that early-stage venture funds under $100M should minimize capital reserves for follow-on investments and instead deploy most capital into new deal opportunities, as the traditional rationales for holding reserves no longer apply in 2026's market dynamics.

## Key Takeaways

- **Evaluate follow-on opportunities** against new investments rather than treating follow-on capital as a separate pool—this forces more rigorous decision-making and increases deal flow diversity.
- **Recycle capital aggressively** by aiming for 100%+ invested funds (Homebrew achieved 120%+) to solve the reserves question while maximizing deployment across more founders.
- **Tier your conviction levels** into three categories—match market pricing (do pro rata), believe less than market (skip or use SPVs), believe more than market (double down early)—rather than defaulting to reserves.
- **Account for changed market conditions** where crowded cap tables, new multi-stage competitors, and auction-like dynamics make historical follow-on data irrelevant and pro rata advantages minimal.
- **Address LP expectations** by showing them the thesis directly and demonstrating how full deployment with recycled capital can drive better returns than traditional reserve strategies.
