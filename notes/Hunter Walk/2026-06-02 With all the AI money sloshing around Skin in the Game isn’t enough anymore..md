# With all the AI money sloshing around Skin in the Game isn’t enough anymore. I’m looking for Heart in the Game too.

**Source**: [Hunter Walk](https://hunterwalk.com/2026/06/02/with-all-the-ai-money-sloshing-around-skin-in-the-game-isnt-enough-anymore-im-looking-for-heart-in-the-game-too/)
**Author**: Hunter Walk | **Date**: Jun 02, 2026

---

## Summary

As capital becomes abundant and financial incentives no longer ensure alignment, the author argues that founders, teams, and investors must demonstrate genuine commitment—'heart in the game'—beyond just financial stakes to build meaningful, long-term focused companies.

## Key Takeaways

- **Evaluate commitment beyond economics**: Look for founders who want their companies on their tombstones and teams staying despite higher-paying offers elsewhere, not just those optimizing for markup and exits.
- **Build double-bottom-line cultures**: Create environments where team members pursue both financial success and meaningful impact on real-world problems, measured by customer outcomes not just capital gains.
- **Question your stakeholder alignment**: Audit whether your CEO, board, and investors actually care about long-term outcomes or are primarily motivated by fees, content marketing, and short-term gains.
- **Use 'legibility to talent' as a filter**: Study how companies communicate their mission and values to attract people committed to the problem, not just the paycheck—as evidenced by transparent blogs and customer-focused narratives.
