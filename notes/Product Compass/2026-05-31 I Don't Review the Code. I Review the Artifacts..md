# I Don't Review the Code. I Review the Artifacts.

**Source**: [Product Compass](https://www.productcompass.pm/p/agentic-engineering-for-pms)
**Author**: Pawel Huryn | **Date**: May 31, 2026

---

## Summary

As AI agents build faster than teams can align, PMs must shift from reviewing code to reviewing artifacts—the decision documents, architecture files, and strategy layers that guide agent work—making building-first prototypes the primary workflow rather than spec-first planning.

## Key Takeaways

- **Review artifacts, not code**: PMs should focus on examining decision documents, architecture files, and strategy layers that explain what was built and why, rather than reading source code or diffs.
- **Build before agreement**: Ship working prototypes in hours or days, then align the team around concrete evidence rather than spending weeks debating specs that will change upon contact with reality.
- **Encode strategy in agent-readable formats**: Place your product strategy in files and documents that agents can read and follow (like strategy.md), not just in your head or in meeting notes, so agents can steer their own work toward your goals.
- **Set clear boundaries for agent autonomy**: Define what agents can and cannot touch through explicit decision documents and contracts, allowing you to delegate confidently without micromanaging every decision.
- **Automate friction that's real and recurring**: Only automate the workflows causing genuine, repeated friction; don't anticipate automation needs that haven't surfaced yet.
