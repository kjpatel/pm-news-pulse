## Summary

John Kim shares how Sendbird transformed into an AI-native organization by treating AI adoption as a product, using internal platforms like Automators and token leaderboards to empower teams to build their own AI tools and track usage across skill levels.

## Key Takeaways

- **Build an internal marketplace**: Create an Automators platform where non-technical teams can request AI tools and internal engineers build solutions, enabling teams like marketing to ship fully functional e-commerce stores with Stripe integration in days without external engineering support.
- **Gamify AI adoption**: Implement token usage dashboards with visible tiers (beginner to "AI God") and quest systems that celebrate wins, making AI usage transparent and motivating across the organization rather than punitive.
- **Make leadership usage visible**: Demonstrate AI adoption from the top down by publicly sharing how executives use AI in their own work, as this is the most powerful adoption signal and permission-giving mechanism for the rest of the organization.
- **Prioritize agency in hiring**: Rewrite job descriptions to emphasize curiosity, agency, and energy over years of experience, attracting people who are naturally inclined to experiment with and adopt new AI tools.
- **Secure templates for production**: Create pre-approved, compliant templates that let non-technical teams safely ship to production without requiring full engineering review, significantly reducing friction in AI tool deployment.
