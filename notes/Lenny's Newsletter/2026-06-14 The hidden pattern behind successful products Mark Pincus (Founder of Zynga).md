## Summary

Mark Pincus, founder of Zynga, reveals his "Proven, Better, New" framework for creating hit consumer products: copy what's proven to work, improve it until it's obviously superior, then add one novel element. His approach emphasizes that instincts are usually right but ideas are often wrong, requiring rigorous testing and ruthlessness about eliminating failed concepts early.

## Key Takeaways

- **Apply the Proven, Better, New framework**: Start by copying proven successful products, make them 10x better so users say "f*ck yes," then add one new element—don't try to innovate on everything at once.
- **Trust your instincts but test your ideas**: Recognize that your instincts are right 95% of the time but your ideas are wrong 75% of the time, so validate assumptions rigorously before committing resources.
- **Kill hope before hope kills you**: Make decisive cuts on underperforming products and features early rather than letting false hope waste time and resources on initiatives that won't succeed.
- **Embrace constraints to unlock ambition**: Being less ambitious about scope actually enables more ambitious outcomes—focus on doing one thing exceptionally well rather than spreading efforts thin.
- **Test at scale with real users**: Don't rely on early feedback or focus groups; launch to real users and measure actual behavior to determine if a product will reach mass adoption.
