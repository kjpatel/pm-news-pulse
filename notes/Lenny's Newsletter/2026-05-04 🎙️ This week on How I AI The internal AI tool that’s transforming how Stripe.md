# 🎙️ This week on How I AI: The internal AI tool that’s transforming how Stripe designs products

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/this-week-on-how-i-ai-the-internal)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky | **Date**: May 04, 2026

---

## Summary

Owen Williams, a design manager at Stripe, built Protodash, an internal AI prototyping tool that enables designers and PMs to create production-quality prototypes in minutes by leveraging Stripe's design system, fundamentally shifting how teams move from documentation to working demos.

## Key Takeaways

- **Bundle your design system with AI**: Connect your company's specific design components and MCP servers to AI code editors like Cursor through well-documented rules, preventing generic 'blurple slop' and ensuring high-fidelity prototypes.
- **Lower the technical barrier for designers**: Remove terminal anxiety by creating tooling that abstracts away Git, npm, and command-line complexity—designers only need to run simple commands and ask Claude questions about technical concepts.
- **Empower PMs as prototyping users**: Design internal tools that let product managers explore ideas and create working prototypes before design reviews, shifting conversations from resource requests to actual product work and improving designer-PM collaboration.
- **Design with real data states**: Build prototyping tools that let you toggle between zero states, edge cases, and different business models in code, enabling faster exploration of real-world scenarios that Figma mockups struggle to represent.
- **Restart quickly when stuck**: Avoid sunk-cost fallacy by clearing context and starting fresh rather than persisting with failing prompts—speed comes from knowing when to reset, not from forcing a single approach to work.
