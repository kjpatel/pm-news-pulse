## Summary

This episode features three hands-on reviews: GPT 5.5's breakthrough capabilities on complex autonomous tasks and hardware hacking, Claude Design's strengths and weaknesses for marketing assets versus product UX, and how a non-coder built Memelord into a $3M API business.

## Key Takeaways

- **Use GPT 5.5 for genuinely hard problems**: Deploy the model on complex technical challenges like multi-hour autonomous migrations and hardware reverse-engineering where its intelligence justifies the $30/million input token cost—not everyday tasks.
- **Structure design systems first in Claude Design**: Import your fonts, colors, and components before generating designs to ensure brand consistency, especially for marketing assets and presentation decks where Claude Design excels.
- **Know Figma still wins on iteration speed**: AI design tools excel at first drafts but traditional tools dominate refinement—Claude Design takes 5-10 minutes per change while Figma offers instant feedback.
- **Give AI creative freedom without constraints**: When you want unexpected results, skip rigid design system constraints and instead provide loose creative direction and reference styles.
- **Spot Claude Design slop by italicized serif fonts**: Recognize AI-generated designs by their telltale signature (italicized serifs everywhere) and explicitly override this pattern in your prompts.
