# How to build a company that withstands any era | Eric Ries, Lean Startup author

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/how-to-build-a-company-that-withstands)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Eric Ries) | **Date**: May 10, 2026

---

## Summary

Successful companies are systematically corrupted by financial pressures and misaligned incentives unless they implement proper governance structures to protect their core mission and values. Eric Ries explores how companies like Anthropic, Costco, and Novo Nordisk stay true to their principles while most venture-backed founders lose control within three years of going public.

## Key Takeaways

- **Implement governance structures early** - Use legal mechanisms like benefit corporation status, mission-aligned board composition, and shareholder agreements to protect your company's values before success creates pressure to compromise them.
- **Understand financial gravity** - Recognize that success makes your company a bigger target for acquisition, activist investors, and pressure to prioritize short-term profits over mission; plan defensive governance accordingly.
- **Align incentives with mission** - Study how companies like Patagonia, Costco, and Anthropic use ownership structures and stakeholder governance to ensure leadership remains accountable to the company's original purpose, not just shareholders.
- **Protect your founding principle with two-page filings** - Establish clear legal protections for your core mission through simple corporate filings that preserve what makes your company valuable and differentiated.
- **Plan for the long term from day one** - Companies that withstand eras share a common trait: they were designed from inception with eternal principles in mind, not just quarterly returns or exit strategies.
