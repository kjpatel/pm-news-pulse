# GPT 5.5 just did what no other model could

## Summary

Lenny Rachitsky shares his hands-on testing of OpenAI's new GPT 5.5 and GPT 5.5 Pro models, demonstrating their superior intelligence and autonomous capabilities through three real-world use cases: building an educational subtraction app, solving technical debt, and reverse-engineering a proprietary Bluetooth device.

## Key Takeaways

- **Evaluate the intelligence tax carefully** - GPT 5.5 Pro's premium pricing is worthwhile for developer and automation tasks, but consider ROI against engineering time before paying for higher-tier models for consumer applications
- **Deploy GPT 5.5 strategically** - Focus initial deployment on tech debt, flaky tests, security backlogs, and long-running autonomous loops where its advanced reasoning creates the most value
- **Master autonomous subagent patterns** - Learn to construct prompts that unlock long-running autonomous loops capable of handling edge cases at scale (98% accuracy across millions of data points)
- **Personalize your model interactions** - Use commands like /personality in Codex to customize the model's tone and working style, moving beyond default behaviors to improve productivity
- **Leverage specialized tools for complex problems** - Combine technical tools (like Bluetooth packet sniffers) with GPT 5.5's reasoning to solve reverse-engineering challenges that previous models couldn't handle
