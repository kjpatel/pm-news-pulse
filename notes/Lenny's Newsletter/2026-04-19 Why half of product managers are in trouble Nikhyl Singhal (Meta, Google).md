# Why half of product managers are in trouble | Nikhyl Singhal (Meta, Google)

**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Nikhyl Singhal) | **Date**: Apr 19, 2026

---

## Summary

Half of product managers face displacement in the next two years as AI fundamentally reshapes the role, requiring PMs to reinvent themselves by embracing AI tools and finding psychological motivation to adapt rather than relying on brand-name resume credentials.

## Key Takeaways

- **Identify your AI moments of joy** - Find specific ways AI enhances your work rather than viewing it as purely a threat; PMs who discover personal enthusiasm for AI tools will differentiate themselves from those experiencing 'smiling exhaustion.'
- **Prepare for organizational restructuring** - Companies will likely shed 30,000 PMs while rehiring 8,000 AI-first product leaders; assess whether your skill set can evolve toward AI-native product thinking or risk displacement.
- **Shed resume dependency** - Stop relying on prestigious company logos for career advancement; focus instead on demonstrable ability to work with AI tools and adaptability, as these will become the new currency for PM hiring.
- **Confront psychological barriers to reinvention** - Acknowledge the mental and emotional resistance to learning new tools and frameworks; seek community support (like Skip) to work through the vulnerability of starting fresh.
- **Master one AI coding tool deeply** - Whether Claude Code, Codex, or Lovable, become genuinely proficient with at least one AI development tool to bridge the gap between product strategy and implementation in an AI-first world.
