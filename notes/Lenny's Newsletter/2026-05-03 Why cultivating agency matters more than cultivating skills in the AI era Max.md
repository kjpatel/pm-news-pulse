# Why cultivating agency matters more than cultivating skills in the AI era | Max Schoening (Head of Product, Notion)

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/why-cultivating-agency-matters-more)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Max Schoening) | **Date**: May 03, 2026

---

## Summary

Agency—the ability to take action and iterate independently—matters more than specific skills in the AI era, as AI has made the first 10% of projects "free" and fundamentally changed how products get built. Max Schoening argues that empowering designers and PMs to ship code, prototype quickly, and embrace AI tools is more valuable than traditional skill development.

## Key Takeaways

- **Prioritize agency over skills**: Invest in hiring and developing people who can take action independently and iterate quickly, rather than those with specific technical skills that AI can now replicate.
- **Leverage the "free 10%" in product development**: Recognize that AI has made the initial phase of every project significantly faster and cheaper—use this to validate ideas and move into implementation quicker than competitors.
- **Get non-engineers shipping code**: Remove barriers that prevent designers and PMs from prototyping in code using AI tools—this compresses feedback loops and accelerates learning across the organization.
- **Design around "tiny cores"**: Build products with minimal, elegant core features (like iPhone's multitouch or Notion's blocks) that create maximal flexibility, rather than trying to ship complete feature sets.
- **Address the software quality gap**: As the volume of shipped software has exploded, quality and thoughtfulness have lagged—this creates competitive opportunity for teams that prioritize intentional design and user empowerment over feature volume.
