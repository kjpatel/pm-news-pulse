# A rational conversation on where AI is actually going | Benedict Evans

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/a-rational-conversation-on-where)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Benedict Evans) | **Date**: May 31, 2026

---

## Summary

Benedict Evans argues that we're in the "1997 of AI"—early and uncertain about the future—and that AI's impact will be comparable to the internet or mobile: massive but not world-ending. The real value will accrue to those who understand distribution, consulting, and how AI augments human work rather than simply replacing it.

## Key Takeaways

- **Reframe the automation question**: Stop asking "What percentage can AI do?" and instead ask "Is this a task or a job?" Jobs involve context, judgment, and relationships that AI augments rather than replaces.
- **Focus on distribution as your moat**: As software becomes easier to build with AI, distribution—not technology—will be the ultimate competitive advantage. Invest in channels and relationships, not just product features.
- **Capitalize on the consulting boom**: The rise of AI implementation services (McKinsey, Accenture, etc.) shows where real near-term value is concentrated. Consider how your business can serve as a translator between AI capabilities and organizational adoption.
- **Prepare for the Jevons paradox**: As AI increases efficiency, demand for those services typically increases rather than decreases. Plan for expansion, not contraction, in your industry.
- **Embrace uncertainty as opportunity**: We're in 1997 of AI—too early to know what wins. Successful organizations will stay flexible, experiment continuously, and avoid overcommitting to specific AI outcomes.
