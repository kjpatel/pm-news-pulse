# How the founder of Morning Brew built a Claude content machine that never runs out of ideas and never sounds like slop | Alex Lieberman

## Summary

Alex Lieberman, founder of Morning Brew and now Tenex, shares how he built a six-step AI-powered Content Machine in Claude that generates publishable posts without sounding like generic slop by mapping workflows, codifying voice through markdown files, and using an interview panel to extract authentic ideas.

## Key Takeaways

- **Map your workflow first** before adding AI—understand your current content creation process end-to-end to identify the actual friction points that AI can solve effectively.
- **Codify your voice in markdown** so Claude drafts in your authentic register rather than producing internet-average content; include specific examples, writing patterns, and stylistic preferences.
- **Use interviews, not drafting, as your AI leverage point**—the slop comes from AI generating ideas ex nihilo, not from writing well; have AI extract your real thinking through structured conversation panels.
- **Turn content creation into a team sport**—use employee advocacy programs like the Tenex Creator Cup with prize pools to make content creation fun and spread your message through your entire organization.
- **Implement a lessons loop** that learns from your feedback on each piece so the system continuously improves its output over time rather than remaining static.
