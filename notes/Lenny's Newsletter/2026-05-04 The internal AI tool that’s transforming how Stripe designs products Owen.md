# The internal AI tool that’s transforming how Stripe designs products | Owen Williams

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-internal-ai-tool-thats-transforming)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Owen Williams) | **Date**: May 04, 2026

## Summary

Stripe design manager Owen Williams built Protodash, an internal AI-powered prototyping tool that enables designers and PMs to create high-fidelity dashboard prototypes without coding, fundamentally transforming how Stripe approaches design reviews and engineering handoffs.

## Key Takeaways

- **Build internal tools with lower bars**: Internal tools don't need to be production-grade to be transformative—Protodash started as Cursor rules and React components before evolving into a full platform.
- **Use design system training in AI tools**: Teach your AI tools your specific design system through Cursor rules and MCPs to prevent "blurple slop" and ensure on-brand outputs.
- **Shift from memos to demos**: Replace design review memos with interactive prototypes in dev boxes to fundamentally change how teams evaluate and iterate on designs.
- **Expect PMs to become power users**: Build prototyping tools flexible enough that product managers naturally adopt them alongside designers, expanding impact across teams.
- **Embed review and testing capabilities directly in tools**: Include design review modes, variant testing, and self-testing screenshots within your prototyping platform to streamline the entire design-to-engineering workflow.
