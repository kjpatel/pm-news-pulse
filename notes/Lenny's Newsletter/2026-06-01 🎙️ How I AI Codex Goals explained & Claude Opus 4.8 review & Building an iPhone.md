## Summary

This episode covers three major AI development stories: how a non-technical recruiter built and shipped an iPhone fitness app using Claude and AI tools, how Codex's Goals feature enables autonomous multi-hour AI work, and a detailed review of Claude Opus 4.8's capabilities and limitations.

## Key Takeaways

- **Use Claude as architect and Claude Code as engineer.** Structure your AI workflow with Claude handling planning and Claude Code handling execution, then use Terminal to deploy—this three-step dance lets non-technical builders ship production code.
- **Debug with screenshots and visual references.** When AI misunderstands your intent, send screenshots, draw what you want, or photograph your starting position rather than editing text prompts—trying different modalities beats staying stuck in one pattern.
- **Write Goals with six key components.** Effective autonomous AI work requires defining outcome, verification, constraints, boundaries, iteration policy, and stopping conditions—this framework ensures Goals actually complete multi-hour tasks successfully.
- **Adapt faster than your expertise gets stale.** The winners in an AI-powered world will be people with humility and curiosity to work with AI in new ways, not those territorial about what they used to do—technical background matters less than adaptability.
- **Test Opus 4.8 carefully for production.** The model excels at greenfield prototypes and design work with excellent voice and token efficiency, but requires thorough testing for production code due to occasional reasoning errors.
