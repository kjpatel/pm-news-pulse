# Why SaaS freemium playbooks don’t work in AI, and what to do instead

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/why-saas-freemium-playbooks-dont)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Vikas Kansal) | **Date**: May 05, 2026

---

## Summary

Traditional SaaS freemium models fail for AI products because free tiers must deliver significant 'magic' to drive adoption, but this magic is expensive to compute. Instead, AI companies should build paywalls around usage intensity, outcomes, and heavy compute modalities to align user value with actual costs.

## Key Takeaways

- **Gate usage intensity over features**: Create tiered access based on usage volume, context windows, and speed rather than model quality. Users understand paying for higher throughput and faster access better than paying for slightly smarter models.
- **Monetize outcomes, not answers**: Paywall features that automate multi-step workflows and save user hours, like AI agents that complete tasks autonomously. Shift from selling 'answers' to selling 'hours saved.'
- **Price compute-heavy modalities separately**: Gate the most expensive features (like real-time interactive models or video generation) behind premium tiers, and consider usage-based pricing for unpredictable high-compute tasks.
- **Ensure free tier delivers aha moment**: Make the free tier good enough that users experience the product's core magic immediately, but design premium tiers to address the economic reality of compute costs rather than artificially restricting features.
- **Avoid pay-per-use bill shock for consumers**: Use predictable subscription tiers with prepaid usage limits instead of AWS-style variable billing, which drives customer frustration and churn.
