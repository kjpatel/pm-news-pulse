## Summary

A non-technical talent recruiter built and shipped a fitness app with AI-generated animal workout videos to the App Store using Replit, Claude, and Gemini—proving that in the AI era, execution is no longer the constraint on turning ideas into products.

## Key Takeaways

- **Use Claude as your technical architect**: Treat Claude like a senior engineer who can guide your app architecture, answer technical questions, and help you navigate decisions—it's faster than learning the fundamentals yourself.
- **Embrace beginner's mindset as an advantage**: Not knowing technical conventions means you'll ask clearer questions, avoid overthinking solutions, and prompt AI tools with literal, specific language that often produces better results.
- **Break complex features into AI-solvable chunks**: Start with static images (Gemini), then layer in video generation separately (Kling/Higgsfield), rather than trying to solve the entire problem at once.
- **Be hyper-literal in your prompts**: Specificity compounds—exact descriptions of what you want (e.g., 'anthropomorphic bear wearing a headband') unlock significantly better AI outputs than vague requests.
