# From a $6.90 newsletter to $3M API: How a non-coder built Memelord | Jason Levin

**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Jason Levin) | **Date**: Apr 27, 2026

## Summary

Jason Levin built Memelord from a $6.90 newsletter to a $3M-funded AI meme creation platform without coding, by leveraging no-code tools like Bubble and embracing an agent-first architecture where AI becomes the primary user.

## Key Takeaways

- **Start simple and validate**: Launch with minimal viable products like newsletters or Google Slides to test demand before building complex infrastructure—Memelord's early $6.90/month newsletter proved product-market fit.
- **Design for agents, not just humans**: Build interfaces optimized for AI agents as primary users rather than traditional UX—the "no UX is the best UX" philosophy unlocks automation potential.
- **Empower non-technical teams with AI coding tools**: Use tools like Cursor to enable marketers and non-engineers to build and iterate independently, creating a "vibe-coding" culture that accelerates creativity.
- **Use free tools as modern lead magnets**: Free utilities generate hundreds of thousands of qualified emails more effectively than traditional PDFs—integrate them directly into your product offering.
- **Build weird hardware projects to inspire product thinking**: Experiment with unconventional AI applications (like bedside keyboards creating tickets) to develop unique perspectives on product possibilities.
