**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Thariq Shihipar) | **Date**: May 18, 2026

## Summary

Anthropic engineer Thariq Shihipar demonstrates how HTML has replaced Markdown as the superior format for communicating with AI agents, enabling richer interactive specifications and living design systems that keep humans meaningfully involved in the development process.

## Key Takeaways

- **Replace Markdown with HTML** for AI agent communication—use interactive elements, visual mockups, and scrollable sections to create plans and specs that are actually readable and engaging rather than thousands of lines of skimmed text.
- **Become a compute allocator, not a code writer**—focus your effort on deciding what's worth building, defining agent boundaries, and maintaining alignment through the planning phase, since the critical skill is directing $500+ of compute efficiently.
- **Build throwaway micro-UIs** for editing specific sections of your plans—create custom interfaces (like gamified editing tools) for particular problems using Claude, then discard them once the task is complete.
- **Encode design systems in HTML** rather than pointing Claude at Figma or repos—maintain a compressed, human and machine-readable HTML file representing colors, typography, spacing, and components that can be passed between projects.
- **Give Claude simple prompts with trust signals**—end instructions with "whatever is needed" to grant creative permission rather than over-constraining with elaborate system prompts that often produce worse results.
