## Summary

Keith Rabois shares hard truths about building in the AI era, including why traditional PM roles are becoming obsolete, how to hire for velocity and adaptability, and why customer feedback can harm consumer product development.

## Key Takeaways

- **Hire for 'barrels' not ammunition**: Focus on identifying and recruiting high-potential individual contributors who can adapt and scale with your organization, rather than hiring for specific skills that may become obsolete in the AI era.
- **Rethink customer feedback loops**: For consumer products, talking to customers can be actively harmful—instead, focus on observing user behavior and building for the smallest viable use case with extreme velocity.
- **CMOs are the new token consumers**: Marketing leaders, not engineers, are becoming the primary consumers of AI tokens—shift your go-to-market strategy and product roadmap accordingly.
- **The PM role is dying**: As AI accelerates development cycles, traditional product management is being replaced by faster iteration, design-led approaches, and data-driven decision making without extensive planning.
- **Prioritize velocity as a core competency**: The best-performing companies right now share three traits: they hire for adaptability, they move faster than competitors, and they can pivot quickly as AI capabilities change.
