# Why we’re at the beginning of the AI hardware boom | Caitlin Kalinowski (ex–OpenAI, Meta, Apple)

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/why-were-at-the-beginning-of-the)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky (featuring Caitlin Kalinowski) | **Date**: May 17, 2026

## Summary

Caitlin Kalinowski, a veteran hardware engineer from Apple, Meta, and OpenAI, argues that we're at the beginning of a major AI hardware boom driven by robotics, with critical bottlenecks around memory costs, humanoid robot deployment readiness, and the convergence of AI with physical systems.

## Key Takeaways

- **Pre-buy memory now.** Startups should secure memory supply ahead of anticipated price shocks, as memory constraints will become a critical gating factor for AI hardware scaling in the near term.
- **Humanoid robots aren't deployment-ready yet.** Despite recent progress, humanoid robots remain prototypes; mass deployment is still gated by fundamental engineering challenges beyond just AI model improvement.
- **Hardware-software co-design is essential.** The most successful AI hardware products will come from teams that deeply integrate hardware engineering with AI systems thinking, not from pure software or pure hardware companies.
- **Watch the robotics category, not VR.** While VR failed to achieve mainstream adoption, the foundational technologies (sensors, compute, manufacturing) now enable practical robotics applications that represent the real next frontier.
