# What launched at Google I/O 2026 (30-minute day 1 recap)

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-launched-at-google-io-2026-30)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky | **Date**: May 20, 2026

---

## Summary

Lenny walks through Google I/O 2026 day one announcements, testing Gemini 3.5 Flash, Anti-Gravity 2.0, Google AI Studio, and new creative tools (Omni, Flow, Stitch, Pomelli) to evaluate which products actually deliver against competitor offerings.

## Key Takeaways

- **Benchmark Gemini 3.5 Flash** against Claude and GPT models on speed and agentic coding tasks before adopting as your primary model—test results show specific performance gaps worth understanding.
- **Test Anti-Gravity's new slash commands** like /grill-me as a more aggressive alternative to Claude Code's clarification flow, particularly for agentic workflows requiring tighter user guidance.
- **Prioritize Google AI Studio's Workspace integration** if internal productivity automation is a key use case, as this appears designed to compete directly for that specific market.
- **Plan for launch-to-availability gaps**—Google's featured products often aren't fully accessible at announcement; verify access before building product plans around new tools.
- **Evaluate creative tools (Omni, Flow, Stitch, Pomelli) for your specific workflow**—test video generation, cinematic editing, UI design, and brand asset generation in real time rather than assuming feature parity with existing solutions.
