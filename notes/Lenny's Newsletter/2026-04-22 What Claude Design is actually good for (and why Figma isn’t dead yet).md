# What Claude Design is actually good for (and why Figma isn’t dead yet)

**Source**: [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-claude-design-is-actually-good)
**Author**: Lenny Rachitsky | **Date**: Apr 22, 2026

---

## Summary

Claude Design and GPT Images 2.0 are powerful AI tools for specific design tasks like landing pages and brand kits, but Figma remains essential for collaborative design system work and complex design workflows.

## Key Takeaways

- **Use Claude Design for** marketing landing pages, slide decks, and creative redesigns—these are the three highest-ROI use cases where AI design tools excel with minimal friction.
- **Expect cost constraints** when using AI design tools at scale; Claude Design's three-variation output is smart UX but budget $200+ for serious projects given token limitations.
- **Leverage GPT Images 2.0** as a breakthrough tool for generating multi-page brand kits and layout exploration using reference images, making it superior to Midjourney for structured brand work.
- **Keep Figma for collaboration** and complex design systems—AI tools can import and structure design systems but can't replace Figma's real-time collaboration and iterative design capabilities.
- **Adopt the DESIGN.md standard** from Google Labs as a portable format for design specifications that works across AI design tools and improves interoperability.
