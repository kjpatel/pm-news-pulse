## Summary

This episode features two elite AI adoption playbooks: John Kim's gamified internal marketplace at Sendbird that treats AI tooling as a product with quests and leaderboards, and Ryan Nystrom's spec-driven development approach at Notion that uses AI agents to automate standups and ship code from Slack mentions.

## Key Takeaways

- **Treat internal AI tooling as a product, not a program** by building gamified marketplaces (like 'quests') where teams earn points, compete on leaderboards, and exchange tokens for rewards—making AI adoption fun and measurable rather than mandated.
- **Provide secure, pre-configured templates for non-technical builders** with authentication, databases, and InfoSec-vetted security pre-built so marketers, sales, and CSMs can ship to production without fear of breaking things.
- **Measure and celebrate token usage across tiers** (Beginner through AI God) to make AI fluency visible, aspirational, and tied to enablement rather than performance reviews.
- **Optimize CI/CD speed to 25% of current time** since fast infrastructure is the mathematical limit on AI agent velocity—agents can iterate 20x more in parallel when tests run quickly.
- **Start with champions and model behavior from leadership** rather than skeptics or mandates; when executives visibly ship faster with AI, adoption spreads organically and signals that this is real and important.
