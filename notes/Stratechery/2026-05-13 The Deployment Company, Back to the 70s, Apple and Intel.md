# The Deployment Company, Back to the 70s, Apple and Intel

**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/the-deployment-company-back-to-the-70s-apple-and-intel/)
**Author**: Ben Thompson | **Date**: May 13, 2026

---

## Summary

Tech companies are rapidly building enterprise deployment divisions staffed with specialized engineers to help customers implement AI systems, mirroring the 1970s mainframe computing era where top-down executive decisions drove business process transformation and workforce replacement rather than user-adoption models.

## Key Takeaways

- **Recognize the 70s computing parallel** - AI enterprise adoption mirrors mainframe computing deployment, not SaaS adoption. Focus on executive decision-makers optimizing business results through process redesign, not on getting rank-and-file employees to use new tools.
- **Plan for data integration challenges** - Successful AI agent deployment requires deep enterprise integration and data unification across scattered applications and storage layers. Budget significant resources for data preparation and ontology work, not just model deployment.
- **Expect layoffs, not augmentation** - Enterprise AI implementation will lead to workforce replacement in specific roles (call centers, data entry, etc.), not productivity augmentation. Frame strategy and communications accordingly with executives and boards.
- **Invest in deployment capability** - The competitive advantage lies in having embedded engineers who can rethink business processes end-to-end for AI integration. Building or acquiring deployment expertise (like OpenAI acquiring Tomoro) is as important as having the model itself.
