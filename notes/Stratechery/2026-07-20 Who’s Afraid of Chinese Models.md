# Who’s Afraid of Chinese Models?

**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/whos-afraid-of-chinese-models/)
**Author**: Ben Thompson | **Date**: Jul 20, 2026

---

## Summary

As AI models become commoditized, success will depend on cost structure rather than capabilities, with Chinese models like Kimi K3 demonstrating that intelligence—not tokens—is the fungible commodity that matters in this new paradigm.

## Key Takeaways

- **Measure intelligence, not tokens**: Different models require vastly different token counts to reach correct answers; focus on COGS per unit of intelligence rather than cost per token, since two models producing the same answer have fungible output despite different token consumption.
- **Build superior cost structures**: In commodity markets, differentiation shifts from features to efficiency; invest in model footprint, inference efficiency, memory efficiency, serving optimization, and token efficiency to compete on COGS rather than capability.
- **Prepare for margin compression**: As intelligence becomes commoditized, prices will converge toward marginal costs set by the lowest-cost producer; companies must either develop structural cost advantages or accept that commodity-like margins will dominate the market.
- **Rethink distribution and bundling strategies**: Traditional tech playbooks around distribution and lock-in lose power when customers can easily switch between equivalent models; focus instead on vertical integration and proprietary data/workflows that create real differentiation.
