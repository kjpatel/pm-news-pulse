# 2026.13: So Long to Sora

**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/so-long-to-sora/)
**Author**: Ben Thompson | **Date**: Mar 27, 2026

---

## Summary

OpenAI's decision to prioritize GPU resources for enterprise AI applications over consumer products has resulted in the discontinuation of Sora, the viral video generation tool that briefly captured public attention in 2025-2026. The move signals OpenAI's strategic shift toward B2B enterprise positioning rather than consumer-facing applications.

## Key Takeaways

- **Understand resource allocation trade-offs**: Companies must choose between consumer adoption and enterprise profitability; OpenAI's decision to reallocate Sora's GPU resources reveals that enterprise applications generate greater strategic value than viral consumer apps.
- **Monitor enterprise pivot signals**: Watch for companies discontinuing consumer products as a leading indicator of their true strategic priorities and where they believe long-term competitive advantages lie.
- **Copyright concerns can kill products**: Sora's legal battles over copyright and training data may have accelerated its demise, suggesting companies must address IP issues early or face forced product shutdowns.
- **Viral adoption doesn't guarantee survival**: A product's brief cultural moment (Sora dominated for ~2 weeks) provides no protection against strategic discontinuation if it doesn't serve core business objectives.
