# The Google Capital Company

**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/the-google-capital-company/)
**Author**: Ben Thompson | **Date**: Jun 02, 2026

---

## Summary

Google's advertising business represents one of the most beautiful business models ever created, but the company's future depends on Google Cloud's ability to generate sufficient capital returns to fund AI infrastructure investments that will determine its competitive position.

## Key Takeaways

- **Understand the capital allocation shift**: Google is transitioning from a pure margin-maximization company (Google Services) to a capital-intensive infrastructure business (Google Cloud), requiring a fundamental rethink of how the company should be valued and what constitutes success.
- **Recognize the competitive necessity of infrastructure spending**: Unlike See's Candies or traditional high-margin businesses, Google must continuously reinvest in expensive AI compute and data centers to maintain its competitive moat, meaning absolute profit generation matters more than relative margins.
- **Monitor Google Cloud's margin expansion trajectory**: The path to Google becoming a sustainable "capital company" depends on Google Cloud achieving 40%+ operating margins while growing 25%+ annually—track these metrics as leading indicators of strategic success.
- **Consider the Berkshire Hathaway parallel for tech valuations**: Just as Berkshire used See's profits to fund BNSF's high-capital growth, Google must now prove that its Services profits can sustainably fund Cloud's infrastructure needs without cannibalizing shareholder returns.
