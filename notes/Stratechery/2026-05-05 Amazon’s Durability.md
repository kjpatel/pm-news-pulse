# Amazon’s Durability

**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/amazons-durability/)
**Author**: Ben Thompson | **Date**: May 05, 2026

---

## Summary

Amazon's decade-long strategy of building massive infrastructure for its own use and then selling it as a service to others—exemplified by the new Amazon Supply Chain Services—demonstrates how the company achieves durable competitive advantages through structural cost efficiency rather than feature differentiation, a pattern that applies equally to AWS and logistics.

## Key Takeaways

- **Build for yourself first**: Create infrastructure and services that solve your own operational needs at scale before packaging them for external customers, which validates the business model and justifies capital expenditures.
- **Optimize for structural cost advantages**: Focus on converting marginal costs into capital costs and then leveraging those investments across multiple customer segments, rather than competing primarily on features or pricing.
- **Think in decade-long timeframes**: Make long-term infrastructure investments that compound over time, accepting short-term inefficiencies to gain lasting competitive moats that competitors cannot quickly replicate.
- **Control your supply chain vertically**: Invest in proprietary infrastructure (chips, networking, logistics) even if initially inferior to alternatives, because this eliminates dependency on suppliers and creates sustainable cost advantages as technology matures.
