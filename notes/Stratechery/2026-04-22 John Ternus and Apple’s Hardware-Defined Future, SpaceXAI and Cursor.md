## Summary

John Ternus's elevation to lead Apple signals a strategic shift toward hardware-defined differentiation, while the SpaceX-Cursor partnership demonstrates how hardware and software integration creates competitive advantage in the AI era.

## Key Takeaways

- **Watch hardware leadership changes** as signals of strategic direction—Ternus's promotion indicates Apple is betting on hardware differentiation rather than services or software-centric strategies going forward.
- **Integrate hardware and software vertically** to create defensible moats—the SpaceX-Cursor deal shows how combining proprietary hardware with software tools creates advantages competitors cannot easily replicate.
- **Recognize the shift from pure software to hardware-software bundles** in competitive positioning—companies competing on software alone will struggle against those controlling both layers of the stack.
