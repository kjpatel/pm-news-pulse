# 2026.24: Hey Siri, Tell Me a Fable

**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/hey-siri-tell-me-a-fable/)
**Author**: Ben Thompson | **Date**: Jun 12, 2026

---

## Summary

Apple shipped competent AI features for Siri at WWDC while Anthropic released Fable 5 with controversial guardrails, demonstrating two different approaches to bringing AI capabilities to market—one focused on proven functionality, the other on safety constraints.

## Key Takeaways

- **Prioritize working demos over vaporware**: Apple's slow but functional Siri AI demos proved execution rather than promises, distinguishing it from failed AI announcements two years prior and demonstrating that competent, reliable AI that leverages existing advantages may be sufficient for market success.
- **Align safety measures with business philosophy**: Anthropic's visible guardrails on Fable 5 reflect the company's values-driven approach, but the silent nerfing of LLM creation and subsequent reversal after public outcry suggests guardrails require transparent communication to maintain credibility.
- **Use AI to amplify existing product strengths**: Apple's approach of enhancing iPhone capabilities through AI—rather than building entirely new experiences—keeps the product focused and leverages the existing ecosystem, potentially maintaining Apple's centrality in computing.
- **Expect predictable patterns in AI safety decisions**: Anthropic's behavior around model guardrails and government relations follows consistent patterns rooted in the company's belief system, making their positioning as "unbeatable" both a strength and source of potential friction.
