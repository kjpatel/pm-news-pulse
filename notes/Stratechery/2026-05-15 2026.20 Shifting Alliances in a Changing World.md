## Summary

This week's Stratechery edition explores how geopolitical and technological alliances are shifting, from Anthropic's compute deal with xAI to the implications of agentic inference reshaping AI infrastructure, and examines US-China relations during Trump's Beijing visit.

## Key Takeaways

- **Distinguish inference types**: Agentic inference (where humans aren't in the loop) will drive different architectural trade-offs than today's answer inference, requiring companies to rethink compute infrastructure priorities beyond raw speed.
- **Follow market signals in AI deals**: The Anthropic-xAI compute deal demonstrates that markets work effectively—pay attention to who's buying compute and from whom as a signal of strategic value and stability.
- **Plan for deployment companies**: AI's real-world impact requires top-down implementation through dedicated deployment companies, not just model development—this is becoming the new competitive battleground.
- **Understand geopolitical positioning**: US-China relations are more nuanced than simple "upper hand" analysis suggests; both sides are incentivized to play for time and stability rather than decisive wins.
- **Monitor Elon's strategic choices**: xAI's infrastructure decisions and space data center plans will shape which companies win in the agentic future—watch whether Musk listens to market signals.
