# Mythos, Muse, and the Opportunity Cost of Compute

**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/mythos-muse-and-the-opportunity-cost-of-compute/)
**Author**: Ben Thompson | **Date**: Apr 13, 2026

---

## Summary

As AI models become increasingly compute-intensive with non-zero marginal costs, hyperscalers face critical opportunity cost decisions about how to allocate scarce GPU capacity between customer services, internal products, and strategic investments rather than competing purely on aggregation and scale.

## Key Takeaways

- **Shift from marginal to opportunity costs**: Recognize that the economics of AI have fundamentally changed from the zero-marginal-cost internet era—compute allocation is now a zero-sum game where serving one customer or use case directly competes with serving another.
- **Prioritize higher-margin workloads**: Follow Microsoft's strategy of allocating GPU capacity first to higher-gross-margin and higher-lifetime-value products (like Copilot) before serving lower-margin customer demand, even if it means sacrificing growth metrics.
- **Invest in differentiation, not scale**: Use constrained compute to build unique internal capabilities and moats (like reasoning models or specialized agents) rather than spreading resources thin across undifferentiated customer services.
- **Reassess customer segmentation**: Develop clear frameworks for which customers and use cases deserve compute allocation based on strategic value and margin contribution, abandoning the aggregation theory assumption that all users should be treated equally.
