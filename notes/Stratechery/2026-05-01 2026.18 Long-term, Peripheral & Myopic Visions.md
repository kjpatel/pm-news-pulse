## Summary

This week's Stratechery bundle highlights Amazon's emergence as a compelling AI infrastructure player, explores the future of AR/VR hardware through new Meta Display glasses, and examines China's reactive rather than proactive geopolitical strategy, particularly through the blocking of Meta's acquisition of Manus.

## Key Takeaways

- **Reassess infrastructure winners** – Amazon's positioning for inference-heavy workloads and new partnerships with OpenAI (Bedrock Managed Agents) signal a shift from training to inference as the dominant AI use case.
- **Hardware possibilities matter** – The Meta Ray-Ban Display demonstrates that AR/VR futures should prioritize practical wearables over immersive headsets, offering a different vision from current Meta Orion prototypes.
- **Understand reactive geopolitics** – China's blocking of Meta's Manus acquisition shows the CCP often responds reactively to perceived threats rather than executing long-term strategic plans, contrary to Western media narratives.
