# Build to Learn vs Build to Earn

**Source**: [SVPG](https://www.svpg.com/build-to-learn-vs-build-to-earn/)
**Author**: Marty Cagan | **Date**: Apr 16, 2026

---

## Summary

The article distinguishes between 'build to learn' (product discovery focused on testing prototypes to validate risks) and 'build to earn' (product delivery focused on creating production-quality products), arguing that modern PMs must excel at building prototypes and developing product sense rather than project management as AI tools have made delivery faster but discovery remains the critical bottleneck.

## Key Takeaways

- **Shift your PM role from project manager to prototype builder** – With AI tools like Claude Code making delivery faster, your value now comes from creating 10-20 prototypes weekly to discover solutions worth building, not from managing feature specifications.
- **Build to learn means testing four key discovery risks** – Use prototyping and customer testing to validate value, usability, feasibility, and viability before moving to production delivery.
- **Leverage live-data prototypes for faster learning** – Modern AI-based tools let you create functional prototypes with real data much cheaper and faster than before, allowing simultaneous testing of multiple approaches instead of sequential iteration.
- **Develop product sense as your core competitive advantage** – While prototyping tools and discovery techniques are learnable, building the judgment to evaluate learnings and guide direction is the hard skill that separates great PMs from facilitators.
- **Embrace the builder/creator identity or risk becoming obsolete** – PMs who see themselves primarily as managers or 'glue' are increasingly at risk; those who embrace building and developing product sense are entering a golden era.
