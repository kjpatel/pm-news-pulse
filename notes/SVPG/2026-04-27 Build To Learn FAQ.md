# Build To Learn FAQ

**Source**: [SVPG](https://www.svpg.com/build-to-learn-faq/)
**Author**: Marty Cagan | **Date**: Apr 27, 2026

---

## Summary

This FAQ clarifies the critical distinction between building to learn (product discovery) and building to earn (product delivery), emphasizing that solution discovery is the hardest and most important work in product development, especially as AI reduces delivery costs. The article redefines product manager responsibilities to focus on ensuring value and viability rather than being a decision-maker or team protector.

## Key Takeaways

- **Reframe your PM role**: Stop thinking of yourself as "the decider" or "the protector" and instead focus on being responsible for value and viability—ensuring customers will buy and business stakeholders' needs are met.
- **Prioritize solution discovery over problem validation**: The hard part isn't confirming the problem is real (your leaders already know it is), it's solving the problem better than alternatives—spend most of your discovery time here.
- **Test solutions against four specific risks**: Build prototypes that systematically test value risk (customer willingness to switch), usability risk (ease of use), feasibility risk (technical difficulty), and viability risk (compliance, security, monetization, marketing feasibility).
- **Use AI to accelerate discovery activities**: Leverage generative AI to help understand problems, rapidly prototype solutions, and test against product risks—different applications than in delivery but equally powerful.
- **Operate as a surgical team, not a hierarchy**: Defer decisions to the team member best suited for each decision rather than centralizing authority, and actively collaborate with cross-functional teams rather than filtering out their input.
