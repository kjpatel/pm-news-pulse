## Summary

Build a Team OS in Claude Code to centralize institutional knowledge and make context discoverable to the entire team, reducing onboarding time and eliminating knowledge silos that trap critical decisions in individuals' heads or buried Slack threads.

## Key Takeaways

- **Centralize institutional knowledge** by creating a shared repository where every team function checks in their context, enabling any team member to query decisions and reasoning in natural language without waiting for a specific person.
- **Reduce context-switching costs** by making information discoverable—teams lose 8+ hours weekly to context questions (lookup + Slack ping + wait + context-switch), which compounds across hiring cycles and creates bottlenecks.
- **Free yourself from being the bottleneck** by building systems that don't require your presence to answer questions, letting you focus on judgment-based work while the team operates independently during offhours.
- **Use a 3-layer architecture** that converges across implementations (DoorDash, Pendo, Google, solo builders), making the pattern portable across Claude Code, Cursor, GitHub Copilot, or any tool using Markdown-based context.
- **Implement in 4 weeks** using a structured approach with adoption playbooks and starter repos, addressing real problems like onboarding speed (6-7 months to settle) and offboarding challenges (47% cite institutional knowledge loss).
