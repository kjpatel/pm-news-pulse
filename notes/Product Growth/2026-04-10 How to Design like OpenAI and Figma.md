# How to Design like OpenAI and Figma

**Source**: [Product Growth](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/ed-bayes-gui-seiz-podcast)
**Author**: Aakash Gupta (featuring Ed Bayes and Gui Seiz) | **Date**: Apr 10, 2026

## Summary

The linear design pipeline is dead—replaced by a bidirectional code-canvas loop where designers and engineers collaborate using AI-powered tools like Codex and Figma's MCP to iterate rapidly on interactive prototypes. This article provides a masterclass on how leading companies like OpenAI and Figma are designing in the AI era with practical workflows and adoption strategies.

## Key Takeaways

- **Replace handoffs with loops**: Adopt the code-canvas loop workflow where designers move fluidly between Figma and Codex, eliminating traditional design spec documentation and engineer handoff delays.
- **Name and annotate with intent**: Establish strong component naming conventions, align design tokens with CSS tokens, and use annotations to ensure AI tools and team members can accurately translate changes between design files and code.
- **Match tools to your design question**: Use Figma canvas for exploring wide ideation and flows, code for testing interactions and edge cases, and toggle between them based on whether you're validating concepts or refining details.
- **Test interactions before engineering**: Build functional prototypes in Codex minutes before engineers touch the work, collapsing the feedback loop from weeks to minutes and catching issues in the design phase rather than QA.
- **Re-evaluate your design maturity**: Assess whether your team is ready for this workflow by checking alignment on naming standards, design system structure, and engineer capability—this isn't a tool swap, it's a process transformation.
