# The PM's Guide to Governance with Eric Ries, author of The Lean Startup

**Source**: [Product Growth](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/eric-ries-incorruptible)
**Author**: Aakash Gupta (featuring Eric Ries) | **Date**: Jul 20, 2026

---

## Summary

Eric Ries explains how governance structures prevent startup collapse and enable long-term value creation, using Costco as a case study and providing actionable frameworks for PMs becoming founders.

## Key Takeaways

- **Implement governance from day one**: Convert to a Public Benefit Corporation at seed stage, write your mission into the charter, and designate a mission guardian to legally protect your company's purpose from investor pressure.
- **Build a cultural fortress, not just legal structures**: Like Costco's Jim Sinegal, resist 'financial gravity' and the temptation of short-term wins (price increases, cost cuts) that create unsustainable forecasting cycles and compromise long-term mission.
- **Use shipping velocity as a trust multiplier**: Fast, consistent product iteration (faster than your industry convention) compounds trust through the 'cooking effect' (users feel safe building on your platform) and 'standardization effect' (your platform becomes the default choice).
- **Structure governance to match your mission, not copy templates**: There is no universal blueprint—your governance should align with your specific mission, ownership structure, funding stage, and company context, not replicate Costco's or Anthropic's approach wholesale.
- **Direct edit AI outputs instead of regenerating**: When using AI tools to write, replace incorrect model outputs with correct versions so the model learns from corrections, reducing the mistake-correction cycle and maintaining your authentic voice.
