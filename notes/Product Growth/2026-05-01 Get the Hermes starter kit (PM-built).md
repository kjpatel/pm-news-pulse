## Summary

Hermes, a self-improving agent framework from Nous Research, is gaining rapid adoption and offers PMs a model-agnostic solution where skills automatically rewrite themselves based on usage patterns, closing the gap between static AI tools and continuously learning systems.

## Key Takeaways

- **Build self-improving skills**: Set up Hermes to automatically rewrite workflows every 15 tool calls based on what worked in your last sessions—your competitive briefing process will optimize from 20 minutes to 8 minutes by week six.
- **Choose model flexibility**: Run the same skills across Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini, or local Llama to avoid lock-in and maintain fallbacks when rate limits or pricing become constraints.
- **Implement distributed agents**: Deploy one agent instance across multiple channels (Telegram, Slack, WhatsApp, Discord, Signal) instead of managing separate tools for each platform.
- **Use the 30-day rollout plan**: Follow the provided week 1, 4, and 8 checkpoints with pre-built SKILL.md templates (competitive-intel, signal-log, decision-log) to systematically improve your PM workflows.
- **Monitor the Monday Gap**: Every week your static prompts stay the same while your competitors' self-improving systems get better—prioritize setup now to avoid falling behind.
