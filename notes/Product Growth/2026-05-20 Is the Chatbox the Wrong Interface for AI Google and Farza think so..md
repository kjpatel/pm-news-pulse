# Is the Chatbox the Wrong Interface for AI? Google and Farza think so.

**Source**: [Product Growth](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/cursor-layer-toolkit)
**Author**: Aakash Gupta | **Date**: May 20, 2026

---

## Summary

The chatbox interface may be a transitional design rather than the final form for AI products; cursor-layer interfaces like Farza's Clicky and Google's Magic Pointer represent a superior paradigm that contextualizes AI assistance directly within the user's workflow.

## Key Takeaways

- **Rethink your interface layer**: Move beyond chatbox-based AI interactions toward cursor-layer designs that operate contextually within the user's existing screen and workflow.
- **Leverage screen context**: Build AI features that can see, understand, and point to specific elements on a user's screen rather than requiring them to describe what they need.
- **Prototype cursor-adjacent features this week**: Test three cursor-layer prototypes in your own product to identify where this interaction model creates the most value for users.
- **Audit your product's UI paradigm**: Use the provided toolkit to systematically evaluate which parts of your product would benefit from cursor-layer interaction patterns instead of modal or chat-based experiences.
- **Speed to market signals validation**: When multiple well-resourced teams ship the same core idea simultaneously (Farza's solo build and Google DeepMind), it indicates the market is ready for this interface shift.
