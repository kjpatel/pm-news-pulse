# How To Measure Development Productivity?

**Source**: [Itamar Gilad](https://itamargilad.com/how-dev-productivity/)
**Author**: Itamar Gilad | **Date**: Jun 21, 2026

---

## Summary

Traditional developer productivity metrics like lines of code, commits, and tokens are misleading activity measures, while output-based metrics encourage waste and misalignment; true productivity should be measured by business and user value outcomes rather than work artifacts or feature velocity.

## Key Takeaways

- **Stop measuring activity metrics** like lines of code, commits, pull requests, or tokens spent—these incentivize volume over value and repeat mistakes IBM made with KLOCs in the 1980s.
- **Reject output-focused planning** that treats product teams as feature factories; optimize instead for outcomes and impact, as 67% of shipped features typically fail to deliver measurable business value.
- **Implement evidence-guided development** with product discovery to reduce waste; simulation data shows outcome-driven teams create 9-10x more value than output-optimizing teams despite slower launch speeds.
- **Realign incentives away from individual developer metrics** toward team-based business outcomes, as measuring developers in isolation creates misalignment and encourages them to optimize for story points rather than user/business value.
- **Measure system health indicators** like work-in-progress levels and product maintenance costs alongside outcome metrics, as high output can create product bloat and organizational drag that undermines long-term productivity.
